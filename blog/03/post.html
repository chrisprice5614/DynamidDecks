The Rings Say Everything

We all remember the method of telling a tree's age. If you cut down a tree and count the rings on the inside, you can determine roughly how many years the tree has been standing. While there's still an ongoing debate on exactly how much time each ring represents, we can be certain that an older tree will have more rings.
Another variable that the rings can show is how easily the tree grew to become the size that it is. When rings are tightly packed together, the tree had to struggle for its survivial. Lack of sunlight, competition and poor weather conditions can cause a tree to take longer to reach a mature size. This longer aging of the tree causes the rings to be packed together in a dense manner. This density makes the wood more strong, durable, and weather resistant.
